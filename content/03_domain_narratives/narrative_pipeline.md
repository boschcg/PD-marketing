# Narrative: Pipeline as a Commercial Decision Engine

In many services firms, the pipeline is treated as a revenue forecasting tool.

Deals are tracked by value, probability, and timing. Success is measured by conversion rate and topline growth. Commercial quality is assessed later, once delivery is underway and margin outcomes become visible.

This approach works at small scale. Under growth, it becomes a source of risk.

---

## Why pipeline decisions matter more than reporting

Every deal that enters the pipeline carries implicit assumptions:
- pricing logic and margin expectations
- delivery model and staffing approach
- duration, complexity, and risk profile
- impact on utilisation and capacity

When these assumptions are not made explicit, the pipeline becomes optimistic by default.

Revenue appears strong, but the commercial consequences remain hidden until execution.

---

## The handover gap

The most common source of margin erosion sits between sales intent and delivery reality.

Deals are sold with:
- optimistic staffing assumptions
- limited visibility into true delivery cost
- incomplete understanding of downstream capacity impact

Delivery teams inherit commitments that are difficult to change, and finance teams explain outcomes that were inevitable once the deal was signed.

The issue is not poor sales execution. It is late commercial visibility.

---

---

## Deal modelling before commitment

Improved pipeline quality comes from modelling deals before they are locked in.

This means:
- testing pricing against realistic delivery assumptions
- understanding margin sensitivity to staffing choices
- assessing delivery risk before scope is finalised
- evaluating how a deal affects firm-level capacity and profit

When these elements are visible early, trade-offs can be discussed explicitly rather than discovered later.

With commercial visibility at the pipeline stage:

- Sales discussions shift from discounting to value
- Delivery concerns are raised before contracts are signed

The pipeline becomes a tool for alignment rather than contention.
